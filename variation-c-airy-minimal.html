Common questions

What is the 60-day CGT reporting rule?

If you sell a UK residential property and make a gain, you must report and pay the CGT within 60 days of completion. We ensure your return is filed well within that window.

Do I need to report if I made a loss?

You're not required to pay CGT on a loss, but reporting it can be beneficial, it can offset future gains. We'll advise on your situation.

How long does the process take?

Most straightforward cases are reviewed and filed within 2–3 working days of receiving your documents. Complex cases may take slightly longer.

I've already missed the deadline, can you help?

Yes. A late report doesn't have to mean a large fine. We'll prepare the return and help you communicate with HMRC to minimise penalties.

What documents do I need?

For property, the completion statement from your solicitor. For shares, a sale confirmation from your broker. We'll tell you exactly what's needed.
