Common questions

Everything you need to know

What is the 60-day CGT reporting rule?

If you sell a residential property in the UK and make a gain, you must report and pay the CGT within 60 days of completion. Missing this deadline can result in automatic penalties from HMRC. We ensure your return is filed well within that window.

Do I need to report if I made a loss?

You're not required to pay CGT if you made a loss, but reporting the loss can be beneficial, it can be offset against future gains. We'll advise you on whether it makes sense to report in your situation.

How long does the process take?

Most straightforward cases are reviewed and filed within 2–3 working days of receiving all your documents. Complex cases with multiple disposals may take slightly longer, but we'll keep you updated throughout.

I've already missed the 60-day deadline, can you still help?

Yes. A late report doesn't have to mean a large fine. We'll assess your situation, prepare the return, and help you communicate with HMRC to minimise any penalties. Don't put it off, contact us today.

What documents do I need to provide?

For property sales, we typically need the completion statement from your solicitor. For shares, a sale confirmation from your broker. We'll tell you exactly what's needed once you've entered the details of your disposal.

Missed the deadline?
We can still help.

Late reporting doesn't have to mean a penalty. Talk to our team and we'll sort it out with HMRC, properly.
